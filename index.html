  • A bit about me.

    I've spent my adulthood living in the most rural places on earth in Africa, the Middle East, and Asia. When I made my first move abroad it was 2003 and communication with my family in the U.S was restricted to once a day when Outlook could be connected and downloaded. Over the years access to satellite communications became more affordable which meant my ability to communicate with my family and friends slightly improved. 2008 was a big year for technology in developing countries. That was when mobile data became widespread which meant I could talk to my loved ones every hour if I so chose, even while living in the desert of Sudan.

    As a teenager, in the 90's, the evolution of the internet and processor speeds mesmerized me. Since then the development of smart devices has kept me dreaming of what's to come . I've been fascinated by computers since my days of playing Pole Position on the Commodore 64. This passion recently convinced me to change careers so that I can fully embrace my enthusiasm for digital technologies.

    Technology excites me, it makes me wonder about the future. I've seen how it can connect people and encourage systematic improvements in remote communities. It's because of digital developers that rural communities around the globe can now connect and warn each other in advance of natural disasters, not to mention numerous other advantages, its truely remarkable!
